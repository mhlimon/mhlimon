Arabic has 28 letters. Every letter connects to the next (except 6 "non-connectors"). Each letter has up to 4 forms depending on position in the word.

{{< rawhtml >}}
<div class="ar">أ ب ت ث ج ح خ د ذ ر ز س ش ص ض ط ظ ع غ ف ق ك ل م ن ه و ي</div>
{{< /rawhtml >}}

Key differences from Bengali:
- Written **right to left** (ডান থেকে বামে)
- No capital letters
- Short vowels (harakat) are often omitted in everyday text

**Practice**: Write each letter 5 times. Focus on the dots — they distinguish letters (ب، ت، ث are all the same base shape, just different dots).
